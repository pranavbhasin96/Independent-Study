  NEW DELHI: Market sentiment got a boost from exit polls predicting easy win for the BJP, in both Gujarat and Himachal Pradesh state elections, as the benchmark equity indices ended near their two-week highs.Sensex rose as much as 375 points during the day in reaction to the exit polls that were released yesterday, but later pared some gains on profit-booking at higher levels. The 30-share index ended with a gain of 216 points or 0.65 per cent at 33,462.97. On the similar lines, Nifty50 closed at 10,333, up 81 points or 0.79 per cent. Sentiment boosted mainly in reaction to the exit polls. Sharp decline in the volatility index also helped in smoothen participants' nerves. With all eyes on actual state elections outcome, we expect fireworks in early trades on Monday. Indications are still favorable, but caution is advisable in stock selection, citing excessive volatility. We suggest not jumping into the trade and waiting for the markets to stabilise, said Jayant Manglik, President, Retail Distribution at Religare Securities. Metal stocks led the gains with stocks such as Vedanta, JSW Steel, MOIL, Hindalco, Coal India, APL Apollo, Jindal Steel and NDMC ending the session with over 2 per cent gains.Nifty Metal closed as the top sectoral gainer at 3,665.90, up 2.66 per cent. Further uplifting the sentiment were realty stocks such as Sobha (up 7.76 per cent), Phoenix (up 7.21 per cent), DLF (up 1.93 per cent), IndiabulLs Real Estate (up 1.36 per cent) and Oberoi Realty (up 0.94 per cent).BSE Midcap index closed 168 points or 1 per cent higher at 16,974.72 with Adani Enterprises, Endurance, MRF, Federal Bank and Mahindra & Mahindra Finance as the top gainers. However, midcaps such as Tata Communications, ABFRL, 3Min India, GlaxoSmithKline, Divi's Laboratories, Reliance Infra ended in red. In the smallcap space, shares of Bank of Maharashtra defied the overall bullish sentiment and plunged 17.79 per cent to Rs 21.25 on BSE. The index however, rallied 247 points or 1.38 per cent to close at 18,170.65. All sectoral barring Nifty PSU Bank index (down 0.20 per cent) ended the day in green. In the primary market, the initial public offering (of Astron Paper & Board Mills got subscribed 45 per cent so far on the first day of the share sale. Meanwhile, shares of multi-specialty hospital chain Shalby Hospitals (down 3.53 per cent), listed at a discount of 3.35 per cent at Rs 239.70 on NSE against the issue price of Rs 248. The scrip listed at Rs 237 on BSE. Globally, sentiment remained weak. European shares were weighed down by disappointing trading updates from fashion brands H&M; and Ferragamo. The pan-European STOXX 600 index was down 0.2 per cent, Reuters reported. At the same time, China's Shanghai Composite index closed down 0.8 per cent at 3,266.1503 points while its blue-chip CSI300 index ended down 1.12 per cent.
