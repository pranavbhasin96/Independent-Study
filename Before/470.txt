  NEW DELHI: The domestic market ended on a positive note on Friday ahead of Gujarat state elections scheduled to start from Saturday amid firm global markets and supportive local cues. BSE Sensex rallied 301 points on Friday to end the session at 33,250.30, up 0.91 per cent and Nifty50 closed the day at 10,265.65, up 99 points or 0.97 per cent. Consumer stocks stole the show with Nifty FMCG surging 2.36 per cent to close at 26,459.55 with stocks such as ITC, UBL, Hindustan Unilever, Godrej Industries, Colpal and Dabur gaining up to 3 per cent. RBI's latest report on bank loan growth also boosted the sentiment. We feel other factors will take a back seat now as all eyes are on Gujarat election. The recent opinion polls indicate the ruling party, BJP, will retain the state, but may see loss of vote share, said Jayant Manglik, President Retail Sales, Religare Securities We suggest keeping leveraged positions hedged, considering the possibility of volatile swings ahead. Nifty has next hurdle at 10350. Continuous participation of banking pack is essential for sustainable surge, added Manglik. Further uplifting the sentiment were pharma stocks such as PEL (up 4.52 per cent), Divis Laboratories (up 3.78 per cent), Sun Pharma (up 2.67 per cent), Cipla (up 1.95 per cent) amd Glaxo (up 1.11 per cent). PSU Bank stocks retreated from Thursdays high levels and the Nifty PSU Bank index ended the day 0.83 per cent lower. Broader markets rallied for a second consecutive session with BSE Midcap index adding 150 points or 0.89 per cent to end at 17,044.36 and Smallcap index closing at 18,211.88, up 1 per cent or 180 points. However, smallcaps such as Hathway (down 7.66 per cent), Pincon (down 6.01 per cent), Panacea Biotec (down 5.70 per cent) and Steel Exchange of India (down 5 per cent) offset gains. In the primary market, the initial public offering (IPO) of Future Supply Chain Solutions got fully subscribed on the final day of the biding process, receiving as many as 3,99,50,680 bids out of a total of 68,49,200, so far. Meanwhile, shares of Panacea Biotec tanked 5.70 per cent, after ratings firm CARE downgraded the firm to 'CARE D', ET NOW reported. According to the ratings firm site, instruments with CARE D rating are in default or are expected to be in default soon. Globally, bitcoin tumbled more than 12 per cent in volatile Asian trading, dropping below the $15,000 level after touching a record high above $16,000 earlier in the session. China's blue-chip CSI300 index was up 0.82 per cent as exports and imports accelerated.
